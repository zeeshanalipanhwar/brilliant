Calling All Powers That Give Two Digits

Number Theory Level 2

Let A,B,CA,B,CA,B,C and DDD be distinct single digit positive integers such that: AB=CD‾ and A+B=C+D A^B =\overline{CD} \text{ and } A+B=C+D AB=CD and A+B=C+D

Compute AB‾+BC‾+CD‾+DA‾ \overline{AB} + \overline{BC} + \overline{CD} + \overline{DA} AB+BC+CD+DA.

Clarification: AB‾\overline{AB}AB denotes a 2-digit integer with AAA and BBB as its digits.


The answer is 220.
