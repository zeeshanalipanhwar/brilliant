2D Hydrophobic-polar protein folding model

Biology Level 2

The above image contains two proteins and their corresponding random conformations based on a hydrophobic-polar protein folding model. The total energy EEE of the proteins can be estimated by summing up the energy values for each type of interactions among the residues using the following rules: E(H−P)=−3,E(H/H)=1,E(H/P)=−2,E(P/P)=−1,E(H-P)=-3,\quad E(H/H)=1,\quad E(H/P)=-2,\quad E(P/P)=-1,E(H−P)=−3,E(H/H)=1,E(H/P)=−2,E(P/P)=−1, where the dashes (−)(-)(−) represent the indirect linear (horizontal or vertical) interactions and the slashes (/)(/)(/) represent the (left or right) diagonal interactions among the residues (((i.e. HHH and P).P).P). For clarification, note that the directed interactions between two residues that are directly connected (as shown by arrows) do not play a part in the estimation of the total energy. Also, note that the interaction between a unique pair of residues can only be considered once. The two lines in the image are hints, not connections.

Can you calculate the total energy values E1E_1E1​ and E2E_2E2​ for both of the above protein conformations? (((Hint: ∣E1+E2∣=28.)|E_1+E_2|=28.)∣E1​+E2​∣=28.)

The lower the total energy value of a protein conformation, the greater the stability.
