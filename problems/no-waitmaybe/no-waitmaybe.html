If "no," then why not?

Note that the three even numbers can be different numbers.

Woah! This problem definitely tricked me when I first saw it! Then I wrote things out algebraically, and it was still tricky to figure out exactly what's going on. It's a wonderful question!

SOLUTION:

An even number is any number of the form 2x2x2x where xxx is any whole number (integer). So the problem asks "Is it possible for 2x2y×2z\frac{2x}{2y} \times 2z2y2x​×2z to equal an odd number?"

Now, let's use algebra:
2x2y×2z=xy×2z =2xzy\frac{2x}{2y} \times 2z = \frac{x}{y} \times 2z\ = \frac{2xz}{y}2y2x​×2z=yx​×2z =y2xz​

This makes it much clearer what needs to happen: yyy must have a factor of 2, so that 2 can cancel out the 2 in the numerator of 2xzy\frac{2xz}{y}y2xz​.

As an example x=3, y=6, z=5\fbox{x=3, y=6, z=5}x=3, y=6, z=5​ will work. Our even numbers are then 2x=62x = 62x=6, 2y=122y = 122y=12, and 2z=102z = 102z=10.

612×10=5 \frac{6}{12} \times 10 = 5 126​×10=5 and 5 is odd, so we're done. However...

GENERALIZATION:

To generalize, consider: 2xzy  \frac{2xz}{y}\ y2xz​  more closely. What does it take in order for the tuple of even numbers {2x,2y,2z2x, 2y, 2z2x,2y,2z} to have the property that 2xzy=2x2y×2z= \frac{2xz}{y} = \frac{2x}{2y} \times 2z = y2xz​=2y2x​×2z= some odd integer?

1) Observe that in order for 2xzy \frac{2xz}{y}y2xz​ to be an integer, yyy must be a factor of 2xz2xz2xz.
2) In order for 2xzy \frac{2xz}{y}y2xz​ to be odd, yyy must be a factor that is itself divisible by the highest power of 2 that 2xz2xz2xz is divisible by.

The easiest way to see the necessity of both of these facts is to consider the prime factorization of all of the numbers (x,y,x, y,x,y, and zzz) involved, and to know that in order for the resulting product to be odd, all of the 2s must cancel.

FOLLOW-UP CHALLENGE:

If x=12x=12x=12 and z=30z=30z=30. What value(s) of yyy will make 2x2y×2z\frac{2x}{2y} \times 2z2y2x​×2z odd?

In other words, can you make 242y×60\frac{24}{2y} \times 602y24​×60 odd?

First, we factor:
x=12=22×3x=12=2^2 \times 3x=12=22×3
z=30=2×3×5z=30=2 \times 3\times 5z=30=2×3×5

As per the argument above, yyy must be a multiple of 22+1+1=24=162^{2+1+1} = 2^4 = 1622+1+1=24=16 that is also a factor of 24×32×5 =7202^4 \times 3^2 \times 5\ = 72024×32×5 =720. So, any of 16, 48, 80, 144, or 720\fbox{16, 48, 80, 144, or 720}16, 48, 80, 144, or 720​ will work.

Let's test y=48y=48y=48.

2x2y×2z=2496×60 =15\frac{2x}{2y} \times 2z = \frac{24}{96} \times 60\ = 152y2x​×2z=9624​×60 =15

Boom! Solved and generalized!
