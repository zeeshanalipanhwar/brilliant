Number Theory Level 1

23+23+⋯+23⏟A copies of 23=45+45+⋯+45⏟B copies of 4520≤A+B≤24A+B= ?\begin{aligned} \underbrace{\dfrac23 + \dfrac23 + \cdots + \dfrac23}_{\color{#20A900}{A}\color{#333333} \text{ copies of } \frac23} &= \underbrace{\dfrac45 + \dfrac45 + \cdots + \dfrac45}_{\color{#3D99F6}{B}\color{#333333} \text{ copies of } \frac45} \\\\\\ 20 \leq \color{#20A900}{A}\color{#333333}+\color{#3D99F6}{B}\color{#333333} &\leq 24\\\\ \color{#20A900}{A}\color{#333333}+\color{#3D99F6}{B}\color{#333333} &=\, ? \end{aligned}A copies of 32​32​+32​+⋯+32​​​20≤A+BA+B​=B copies of 54​54​+54​+⋯+54​​​≤24=?​

I have A\color{#20A900}{A}\color{#333333}A copies of 23\frac2332​ on the left-hand side of the equality, and B\color{#3D99F6}{B}\color{#333333}B copies of 45\frac4554​ on the right. In total, I've written down between 20 and 24 fractions. Exactly how many fractions are there?

For both fractions I made their denominator equal, so:

23=1015\frac{2}{3} = \frac{10}{15} 32​=1510​ and 45=1215\frac{4}{5} = \frac{12}{15} 54​=1512​ .

The smallest multiple they have on common is 60, so to get equality this means we need 6 times 23\frac{2}{3} 32​ and 5 times 45\frac{4}{5} 54​. Hence the answer must be a multiple of 11, so the answer is 22.

The first clue is that the response options are whole numbers.

The smallest whole number to be made of 2/3 is 2 (3 sets), and the smallest while from 4/5 is 4 (5 sets). The least common multiple of 2 and 4 is 4. That's 6 A = 5 B (aka 4=4) and 6+5=11. That means we need the next common multiple (8). 12 A = 10 B (aka 8=8) and 12+10=22, which satisfies the inequality.
